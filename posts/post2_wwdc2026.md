# Adaptive UI probably doesn't need a large language model

One thought after WWDC:

Many developers are already thinking about how to integrate Foundation Models into their apps.

But for adaptive interfaces, a large language model may not even be necessary.

If an app knows:

* what I use most often
* what I used recently
* what I never touch

it can already make better decisions about what to show.

The first generation of adaptive UI might be powered more by relevance scoring than by prompts.

AI could come later.

Sometimes the smartest interface is just one that pays attention.
