# Maybe the most interesting AI feature from WWDC isn't a chatbot

After WWDC 2026, I keep thinking about one question:

What if the most interesting use of Apple's new AI frameworks isn't generating text?

Most discussions focus on Siri AI, chat experiences, and prompts.

But I'm more interested in something else:

Can AI help simplify interfaces?

Imagine an app that learns which actions matter to you and gradually reduces visual noise without removing functionality.

Not a chatbot.

Not a new screen.

Just a better understanding of what deserves attention.

WWDC gave us Foundation Models, Core AI, App Intents, and Personal Context.

I wonder if the next generation of apps will use them to adapt interfaces rather than replace them.
